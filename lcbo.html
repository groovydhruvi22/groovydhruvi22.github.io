Company Background:
LCBO stands for the Liquor Control Board of Ontario. It is a government-owned organization responsible for the sale and distribution of alcoholic beverages in the province of Ontario, Canada. The LCBO operates retail stores, as well as an online platform, offering a wide range of alcoholic products including wine, beer, spirits, and other related items. It regulates and controls the sale, purchase, and consumption of alcoholic beverages in Ontario while promoting responsible drinking.
Problem
Solution
The LCBO is facing a significant problem of increasing theft of select products from three categories, which has resulted in a loss of approximately $77 million in 2018. The repeated occurrence of theft of these select products poses a threat to the financial stability and reputation of the LCBO. Furthermore, the increasing frequency of theft incidents raises concerns for the safety of store employees. Therefore, there is an urgent need to identify the root cause(s) of this problem and develop effective solutions to mitigate the impact of theft on the LCBO's revenue, reputation, and employee safety.
Our proposed solution is to implement an in-store ordering system to reduce the theft of select products in three categories that are causing significant financial loss. This solution involves keeping the most stolen products in storage and providing customers with access to an iPad-based ordering system, allowing them to place orders for these products. The store staff will then bring the requested products to the customer service desk, ensuring that the products are not accessible to potential thieves. This solution addresses the root cause of the problem and provides a safer shopping experience for customers and employees, while also protecting the financial interests and reputation of the LCBO.
EMPATHIZING WITH THE USER
During this project, it was crucial to prioritize the needs of end users — LCBO's customers. Simultaneously, as a designer, it was equally vital to comprehend and address the specific requirements and objectives of the company. Striking a balance between user-centric design and aligning with the business goals ensured a successful outcome.
Understanding the End User:
While developing the in-store requesting app, I recognized that it was going to impose a burden on our customers. However, I made it a priority to keep this in mind throughout the design process, aiming to minimize any inconvenience or challenges they might face. ​

During the project, it was surprising to discover that a significant portion of LCBO's customer base consisted of older individuals who may not be as comfortable with technology. This insight played a crucial role in designing the in-store requesting app. To cater to their needs, I focused on keeping the user interface intuitive and the user flow as straightforward as possible. By prioritizing ease of use, I aimed to make the app accessible and inclusive for all users, regardless of their level of tech-savviness.
GOALS
Company Goals:
  • Reduce theft: Develop a system that effectively addresses and minimizes incidents of theft within the store environment.
  • Customer satisfaction: Ensure that the solution caters to customers' needs and preferences while maintaining a positive shopping experience.
User Goals:
  • Quick and easy product requesting process: Create a seamless and efficient system that allows users to request products in-store with minimal effort and time.
  • Simplicity and minimal user flow: Design a user-friendly interface and intuitive user flow that simplifies the requesting process, minimizing any complexity or confusion. This ensures a smooth and seamless shopping experience for the users.
